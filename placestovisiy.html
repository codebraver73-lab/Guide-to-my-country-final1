Germany's monuments:

  1. Neuschwanstein Castle (Bavaria)

    • Description: One of the most iconic castles in the world, built by King Ludwig II in the 19th century.
    • Why Visit: Its fairy-tale design inspired Disney’s Sleeping Beauty Castle.
    • Highlight: Surrounded by the Bavarian Alps and stunning lakes — perfect for photos.

  2. Brandenburg Gate (Berlin)

    • Description: A neoclassical monument built in the 18th century, symbolizing peace and unity.
    • Why Visit: It became a symbol of Germany’s reunification after the fall of the Berlin Wall.
    • Highlight: Beautifully lit at night, located near major historical landmarks.

  3. Berlin Wall Memorial (Berlin)

    • Description: Remnants of the wall that divided East and West Berlin from 1961 to 1989.
    • Why Visit: A powerful reminder of Cold War history and the fight for freedom.
    • Highlight: The East Side Gallery — a section covered with political and artistic murals.

  4. Zwinger Palace (Dresden)

    • Description: A magnificent Baroque palace built in the 18th century by Augustus the Strong.
    • Why Visit: Home to world-class art collections and stunning gardens.
    • Highlight: The Old Masters Picture Gallery, featuring works by Raphael and Rembrandt.

  5. Reichstag Building (Berlin)

    • Description: The seat of the German Parliament (Bundestag), rebuilt after WWII.
    • Why Visit: Combines historic architecture with a modern glass dome by architect Norman Foster.
    • Highlight: Visitors can go inside the glass dome for panoramic views of Berlin.

  6. Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom, Cologne)

    • Description: A massive Gothic cathedral and UNESCO World Heritage Site, started in 1248.
    • Why Visit: It’s one of the tallest churches in the world and took over 600 years to complete.
    • Highlight: Climb the 533 steps for a breathtaking view of the Rhine River.
